WHAT YOU NEED

You will need some of the following to make great pasta:

  • Pasta: can be any type, long, short, curly, whatever
  • Tomato sauce, if you want a marinara style sauce.
  • Fresh tomatoes, if you have them. Excellent in red sauces or in creamy pastas when added at the end of the cooking process
  • Some fat, preferably olive oil or butter
  • Fresh vegetables like onions or shallots, garlic, carrots
  • Frozen veggies are fine too. Include frozen peas and corn, diced carrots, lima beans, it's all good.
  • Fresh herbs if you have them, but who are we kidding. This is weeknight pasta.
    Dried are great too:
    basil, thyme, oregano
  • Spices like black pepper, smoked paprika, curry powder, cayenne
  • Sauces like sriracha and soy sauce can enhance flavor. Be careful not to overuse
  • Salt
  • Sugar

You don't need everything on this list. Not by far. Simple is best right?

Instead, imagine how you want your pasta to be. Veggie-filled marinara? Creamy fettucine? It's your choice.

See below for step by step instructions on how to cook world-class tasting pasta in 20 minutes or less, on a week night.

WHAT YOU DO

For the sauce

  1. Begin boiling water
  2. Chop whatever veggies you've decided to use, starting with onions
  3. Turn on pan, bring to medium heat before adding any fat
  4. Begin to sautee onions on medium heat in butter and/or olive oil
  5. Add chopped garlic once onions begin to become transluscent
  6. Add any other veggies to your sautee pan
  7. Incorporate fresh tomatoes, if using, after veggies have had a chance to sputter and fry a little. The browner they are, the more delicious caramel notes you'll enjoy in your sauce
  8. Incorporate tomato sauce/canned tomatoes, if using
  9. Once veggies are beginning to become tender (and a little bite to them is nice!), take a spoonful of sauce and let it cool to taste it
  10. Assess flavor, adjust saltiness, add sugar in tiny increments if necessary, adjust heat and spiciness
  11. Let simmer for 5 minutes before taking off heat. This isn't your grandma's bolognese that needs 15 hours cook time
  12. Finish with butter or cream if desired. Adding cream to very warm tomato based sauces may lead to curdling
  13. Set aside until pasta is done

    14. For the pasta, which you should do once the veggies in your sauce have already started sauteeing and will be tender soon.

    1. Bring water the a boil. Salt it.
    2. Place pasta in the water, gently to avoid splashes.
    3. Pro Tip: set a timer
    4. Taste it when timer goes off. You might like it "al dente," which means with bite, or chewy
    5. Drain pasta in collander or with lid of pot
    6. Place pasta back in the pot you used for boiling,
    7. Add butter or olive oil (preferably butter) to the cooked pasta, stir to incorporate
    8. Mix desired amount of pasta with desired amount of sauce
    9. ENJOY! You've just made delicious weeknight pasta, like a boss.
